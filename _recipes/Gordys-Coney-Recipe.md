ingredients:
- 2 lbs ground beef
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 cups beef broth
- 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tbsp yellow mustard
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 12 hot dogs, grilled or steamed
- 12 hot dog buns
- diced onion and shredded cheddar for topping

directions:
- Brown the ground beef and diced onion in a large pot over medium heat, breaking the meat into very fine crumbles.
- Drain excess grease, then stir in garlic and cook for 1 minute.
- Add beef broth, tomato sauce, tomato paste, mustard, Worcestershire, chili powder, paprika, cumin, pepper, salt, and brown sugar.
- Bring to a low simmer and cook uncovered for 35 to 45 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
- Taste and adjust salt or chili powder as needed.
- Place hot dogs in buns, top with coney sauce, diced onion, and cheddar if desired.
---

This one is best when the sauce simmers low and slow. The texture should be saucy but still spoonable.
